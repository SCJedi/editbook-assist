# USPS Rural Carrier Case Equipment Specifications

## Research Findings Summary

This document compiles findings from web research combined with insider knowledge from an active USPS rural carrier. It distinguishes between CONFIRMED specifications (from official USPS sources), USER-PROVIDED details (from carrier experience), and areas that remain UNKNOWN or require clarification.

## 1. Equipment Inventory - Cell Counts and Specifications

### Item 124-D: Main Carrier Case
- **Type**: Primary carrier case
- **Total Cells**: 240 separations (1" wide each)
- **Shelves**: 6 shelves
- **Cells Per Shelf**: 40 cells (240 √∑ 6 = 40)
- **Total Width**: 240 inches of usable casing space
- **Features**: Removable dividers; wider separations can be configured for high-volume addresses
- **Source**: CONFIRMED - USPS Handbook PO-603 (Rural Carrier Duties and Responsibilities)

### Item 143-D: Swinging Wing Case
- **Type**: Wing case (swings/folds)
- **Total Cells**: 120 separations
- **Shelves**: 6 shelves (standard for all rural case equipment)
- **Cells Per Shelf**: 20 cells (120 √∑ 6 = 20)
- **Total Width**: 120 inches of usable casing space
- **Source**: CONFIRMED - USPS Handbook PO-603

### Item 144-D: Wing Case with Table
- **Type**: Wing case with integrated work surface (desk)
- **Total Cells**: 240 separations (1" wide each)
- **Shelves**: 6 shelves
- **Cells Per Shelf**: 40 cells (240 √∑ 6 = 40)
- **Total Width**: 240 inches of usable casing space
- **Features**: Includes desk/table surface for carrier work area
- **Source**: CONFIRMED - USPS Handbook PO-603

### Standard Rural Route Configuration
- **Typical Setup**: 3 pieces of equipment per route
- **Total Standard Capacity**: 720 cells (3 √ó 240 = 720 one-inch slots)
- **Reserved Cells**: Approximately 20 cells reserved for administrative purposes (A-Z rework, CFS, etc.)
- **Net Usable**: 700 cells for address assignments on a "normal" route
- **Source**: CONFIRMED - Rural Mail Talk forums citing PO-603 standards

### Legacy Equipment (Previously Authorized)
These items are older standards, included for reference:
- **Item 125**: Carrier Case, 100 separations (2.25" wide)
- **Item 126**: Carrier Case, 208 separations (2.5" wide)
- **Item 140**: Wing Case, 63 separations (2.125" wide)
- **Item 145**: One-Bundle Sliding Shelf Case, 160 separations (1" wide)
- **Item 146**: One-Bundle Sliding Shelf Case, 230 separations (1")
- **Source**: CONFIRMED - Various USPS equipment catalogs

## 2. Physical Layout (User-Provided)

### Three-Wing U-Shape Configuration
- **Middle Wing**: Item 144-D (Wing Case with Table) ‚Äî 240 cells, includes desk surface
- **Left Wing**: Typically Item 124-D or 143-D ‚Äî folds in toward center
- **Right Wing**: Typically Item 124-D or 143-D ‚Äî folds in toward center
- **Optional Add-On**: Sometimes a full or half wing is added on the right side
- **Carrier Position**: Carrier stands in the center of the U, facing the middle wing
- **Source**: USER-PROVIDED

### Shelf Configuration
- **6 shelves per wing** (universal across all equipment types)
- **Shelves numbered**: Bottom to top (shelf 1 = bottom, shelf 6 = top)
- **Source**: USER-PROVIDED + CONFIRMED in PO-603 references

### Casing Direction (Delivery Sequence Flow)
- **Start**: Bottom-left cell of the left wing
- **Pattern**: Serpentine/spiral upward
  - Shelf 1 (bottom): Left ‚Üí Right
  - Shelf 2: Left ‚Üí Right (or possibly Right ‚Üí Left in true serpentine)
  - Continues upward through shelf 6 (top)
- **End**: Top-right cell of the right wing
- **Equipment Boundary Constraint**: An address cell MUST NOT split across equipment boundaries (e.g., a 4" address cannot be 2" on one wing and 2" on the next)
- **Source**: USER-PROVIDED
- **Status**: PARTIALLY CONFIRMED - Research confirms delivery sequence casing, but exact left-right serpentine pattern details need verification

### Casing Pattern Variations
Research revealed two common organizational approaches:
1. **"Carousel" Pattern**: A1, B1, C1, A2, B2, C2, A3... (horizontal across wings, then down)
2. **"Normal" Pattern**: A1, A2, A3, A4, A5, B1, B2, B3... (vertical within sections, then next section)
- **Source**: CONFIRMED - Rural Mail Talk forums, USPS training materials
- **Note**: The user's described bottom-left ‚Üí spiral up ‚Üí top-right matches the "Normal" pattern

### Management Discretion
- **Case setup is determined by management** when route is created
- **Changes allowed when**:
  - Regular route is vacant
  - Route changes regular carriers (with prior notification)
  - Anytime on auxiliary routes
  - Equipment becomes unserviceable
  - By mutual agreement between carrier and management
- **Source**: CONFIRMED - NRLCA documentation, M-38 handbook

## 3. Reserved Cell Map - Form 3982 and Mail Status Codes

### Form 3982: Customer Special Instructions
- **Full Name**: PS Form 3982 (city carriers) / PS Form 3982-R (rural carriers)
- **Purpose**: Records customer address changes, special delivery instructions, and boxholder requests
- **Cell Placement**: ‚úÖ CONFIRMED ‚Äî **First 1" cell of each shelf row** (start of each row)
- **Width**: 1 inch per cell
- **Total Form 3982 cells**: **6 for the entire case** (one per shelf row, at the very start of the left wing)
- **NOT per wing** ‚Äî only at the start of each row (which begins on the left wing)
- **Source**: USER-CONFIRMED

## 4. Cell Sizing Rules and Constraints

### Base Cell Unit
- **Standard width**: 1 inch per cell
- **Adjustability**: Dividers are removable; cells can be widened
- **Multi-cell addresses**: An address can span multiple cells (e.g., 2", 4", etc.) for high mail volume
- **Source**: CONFIRMED - PO-603

### Equipment Boundary Constraint
- **Rule**: An address cell MUST NOT split across equipment boundaries
- **Example**: A 4" address cannot be 2" on left wing and 2" on middle wing
- **Implication**: When calculating cell layout, addresses nearing equipment boundaries must either:
  - Fit entirely on current wing, OR
  - Begin on the next wing
- **Source**: USER-PROVIDED

### Cell Size Variation by Route
- **Standard**: 1" per address
- **Alternate configurations**:
  - 2" separations with two deliveries per separation
  - Variable widths based on mail volume characteristics
  - Some routes have been compressed (e.g., 70 addresses in 7 slots = ~2" wide multi-address cells)
- **Management discretion**: Managers may vary separation size and deliveries per separation based on route mail volume
- **Source**: CONFIRMED - M-38 handbook, Rural Mail Talk forums

### Letter vs. Flat Mail
- **Letter mail**: Cased vertically (standard cell arrangement)
- **Flat mail**: Cased horizontally in wider separations (10" slots mentioned in PO-603)
- **Source**: CONFIRMED - M-41, M-38 handbooks

## 5. Casing Flow Pattern - CONFIRMED

### ‚úÖ Flow Pattern (User-Confirmed)

The casing flow goes **horizontally across all wings on the same shelf**, then moves up to the next shelf:

```
SHELF 6: [Left Wing Row 6] ‚Üí [Middle Wing Row 6] ‚Üí [Right Wing Row 6]  ‚Üê END (last address)
SHELF 5: [Left Wing Row 5] ‚Üí [Middle Wing Row 5] ‚Üí [Right Wing Row 5]
SHELF 4: [Left Wing Row 4] ‚Üí [Middle Wing Row 4] ‚Üí [Right Wing Row 4]
SHELF 3: [Left Wing Row 3] ‚Üí [Middle Wing Row 3] ‚Üí [Right Wing Row 3]
SHELF 2: [Left Wing Row 2] ‚Üí [Middle Wing Row 2] ‚Üí [Right Wing Row 2]
SHELF 1: [Left Wing Row 1] ‚Üí [Middle Wing Row 1] ‚Üí [Right Wing Row 1]  ‚Üê START (first address)
```

### Row-by-Row Breakdown (Standard 3-Wing: 124-D + 144-D + 124-D)

Each row spans all wings on the same shelf:
- **Left Wing (124-D)**: 240 √∑ 6 = **40 cells per row**
- **Middle Wing (144-D)**: 240 √∑ 6 = **40 cells per row**
- **Right Wing (124-D)**: 240 √∑ 6 = **40 cells per row**
- **Total cells per row**: 40 + 40 + 40 = **120 cells per row**
- **Total rows**: 6
- **Grand total**: 720 cells

### Row 1 (Bottom Shelf) Special Layout
```
[3982][--------- addresses ---------][Left Wing end] ‚Üí [--------- addresses ---------][Middle Wing end] ‚Üí [--- addresses ---][MACH|NON-MACH|UTF|IA|NSN|ANK|OTHER]
  1"         39 cells                                          40 cells                                      32 cells            1" | 2" |1"|1"|1" |1" | 1"
```

- Form 3982: 1" cell at START of row (left wing, cell 1) ‚Äî **1 cell on Row 1**
- Reserved status codes: last 8 cells of the row (end of right wing) ‚Äî **8 cells**
- Usable address cells on Row 1: 120 - 1 (Form 3982) - 8 (status codes) = **111 cells**

### Rows 2-6 Layout
```
[3982][--------- addresses ---------][Left Wing end] ‚Üí [--------- addresses ---------][Middle Wing end] ‚Üí [--------- addresses ---------][Right Wing end]
  1"         39 cells                                          40 cells                                             40 cells
```

- Form 3982: 1" cell at START of row (left wing, cell 1) ‚Äî **1 cell per row**
- Usable address cells per row (Rows 2-6): 120 - 1 = **119 cells each**
